# GMEPay+ — Finance / Compliance / Platform services: plain-language digest

Survey date: 2026-07-01. Read-only. Sources: `Documentation/services_backlog/<svc>.md` + skim of `services/<svc>/src/main/java`.
All services follow the MSA rule: own their DB, talk to peers only via API or async event (never cross-DB reads). Phase-1 messaging is a transactional **outbox** with an `EventPublisher` seam; Kafka topics (`gmepay.<eventType>`, e.g. `gmepay.payment.approved`) are wired where consumers exist.

---

## 1. What each service does (and who it talks to)

**settlement-reconciliation** — The money-out engine for ZeroPay. It classifies every transaction as **NET** (domestic/LOCAL partner: GME keeps its share, remits payout minus merchant fee) or **GROSS** (international/OVERSEAS partner: GME remits the full payout and invoices the merchant monthly). It builds the outbound ZeroPay files — ZP0061 (morning request), ZP0063 (afternoon), ZP0065 (payment detail), ZP0066 (refund detail) — sends them by SFTP, then reconciles the inbound result files (ZP0062/ZP0064) line-by-line against what it sent, raising `reconciliation_item` discrepancies and ops alerts on any mismatch. It books each partner's settlement liability rounding **once** at the output amount (partner-configured rounding mode, Addendum-001) and computes the rounding **residual** for the ledger. It also runs daily tie-out jobs (USD pool identity, aggregate SUM/count vs scheme totals, refund netting) and generates monthly tax invoices for gross partners. Talks to: transaction-mgmt (reads committed txns via API/event), config-registry (partner type, rounding, fee config), prefunding (debit tie-out), scheme SFTP; emits `settlement.completed`; pushes rounding-residual + settlement journals to revenue-ledger.

**revenue-ledger** — The double-entry books. It **consumes `payment.approved`** (Kafka consumer + idempotent sync `POST /v1/revenue/capture`) and writes one revenue-capture row per transaction (FX margin = collection_margin + payout_margin, plus service charge). It computes the scheme fee split — gross merchant fee − VAN fee = net fee, then the configurable GME/scheme share (70/30 for ZeroPay, `floor` rounding so the two sides always sum to net exactly) — and posts balanced journals via `LedgerPostingService`/`JournalStore` (rejects unbalanced journals). It captures the settlement rounding residual as its own journal line (gain/loss) so partner books tie to internal books. Exposes `/v1/revenue`; consumes `payment.approved` and `settlement.completed`.

**reporting-compliance** — The regulatory reporting lane. Builds **BOK FX1014/FX1015** exports (cross-border only; domestic same-currency txns are exempt), **KoFIU** CTR/STR AML feeds, and **Hometax** tax-invoice submissions for the monthly gross-settlement merchant fee. It aggregates revenue reports (daily/monthly, FX-margin vs scheme-fee streams, KRW-equiv conversion at the treasury rate) and the settlement-batch status view. Real aggregation is built; the actual government filing channels (formats/SFTP/mTLS) are externally gated (OI-02/OI-03). Consumes revenue-ledger + transaction-mgmt; exposes `/v1/reports` + BOK export; has BOK/Hometax schedulers and stub gov clients.

**config-registry** — Source of truth for schemes, partners, rules, margins, treasury rates. Config-not-code: adding a scheme/partner/rule is a DB insert, served through cached registry ports (`Scheme/Partner/Rule/TreasuryRatePort`) that consuming services inject. It owns the **partner onboarding wizard** (SCD-6 versioned partner aggregate), the `partner_kyb` child aggregate + screening trigger, partner credentials (bcrypt api-key + Vault HMAC secret) with a **rotation scheduler**, mTLS certs, IP allowlists, commission-share config, credit limits, and prefunding config. Every write is 4-eyes change-request-gated and lands an append-only, hash-chained **audit_log** in the same transaction; changes publish via config outbox. Exposes `/v1/schemes|partners|rules|treasury-rates`; **calls kyb-adapter** (screening), **notification-webhook** (webhook provisioning), and **prefunding** (credit-limit push).

**auth-identity** — The identity/credential guard. Verifies partner **HMAC-SHA256** signed requests (canonical string + timestamp-drift 300s + Redis nonce replay guard 600s) at the gateway, issues/verifies operator **OAuth2/JWT** with RBAC, handles mTLS and key lifecycle, and exposes `/internal/auth/verify` + `/internal/v1/credentials/resolve` (returns partnerId + Vault HMAC secret to the gateway over the internal network). Consumes partner credentials **from config-registry**. Own append-only audit_log for credential issue/rotate/auth-fail.

**notification-webhook** — Partner webhook delivery. **Consumes `payment.approved`** (+ `payment.*`, `settlement.completed`, `prefunding.low`), builds a typed signed envelope (HMAC-SHA256 header, ISO timestamp, ULID event-id, per-partner Vault signing secret), POSTs over HTTPS-only with a 10s timeout, and runs the **outbox poller** retry ladder (0/30/120/600/1800s, then 3600s ×5) → **DLQ** after 10 failures with a P2 ops alert. Filters by each partner's event-type subscription. Exposes webhook config + DLQ review/retry API; writes transaction-trail step 7 (WEBHOOK_QUEUED) and step 8 (WEBHOOK_DELIVERED).

**merchant-qr-data** — Merchant/QR mirror + validation. Owns the canonical merchant/qr_code/franchise store (Postgres) plus a MongoDB projection for fast `GET /v1/merchants/{qr}` lookups at pay time. Ingests ZeroPay sync files (ZP0041–ZP0055) via the scheme adapter using staging→diff→atomic-promote (full-sync failure rolls back, live data untouched), ShedLock-guarded so only one pod runs the daily sync. Consumes scheme-adapter sync; exposes merchant/QR validation.

**ops-partner-bff** — Backend-for-frontend for admin-ui + partner-portal-ui. Aggregates ~19 endpoints over ~10 upstream services (config-registry, prefunding, rates, reporting, rbac-admin, approval-queue, audit, api-key, etc.). Enforces JWT + partner-scoping (a partner JWT can only read its own partnerId; admin bypass), proxies login to auth-identity. Stubs only under the `demo` profile; `live` profile uses REST clients.

**kyb-adapter** — Stateless vendor-agnostic KYB layer. Exposes `POST /v1/kyb/screen` + `GET /v1/kyb/result/{ref}`; behind the `KybProvider` port sit a deterministic **Stub** (default/CI), the **Octa** sanctions/PEP vendor adapter (Resilience4j circuit-breaker + retry, Vault creds), and a **business-registration verifier** (KFTC / stub). It owns no DB — screening outcomes persist in config-registry's `partner_kyb`. Called synchronously by config-registry during the onboarding wizard's step-3.

## 2. Key coordinated flows (business language)

1. **Payment approved → revenue booked.** payment-executor emits `payment.approved` (`gmepay.payment.approved`). **revenue-ledger** consumes it and idempotently books a revenue-capture row (FX margin + service charge) and the 70/30 scheme-fee-split journal (gross fee − VAN = net; GME share `floor`-rounded, scheme gets the remainder; balanced double-entry). In parallel **notification-webhook** consumes the same event and enqueues a signed webhook to the partner.

2. **Settlement booked → journal + rounding residual (once).** settlement-reconciliation selects the window's APPROVED txns, splits NET vs GROSS, sums at full precision, then **books the per-partner liability rounding exactly once at the output** (partner rounding mode). The `residual = precise − booked` (gain/loss) is posted as a dedicated REVENUE_ROUNDING journal in revenue-ledger so partner books tie to internal books. Batch files (ZP0061/63/65/66) go out by SFTP and `settlement.completed` is emitted.

3. **Scheme result files → reconciliation.** Inbound ZP0062/ZP0064 are matched per-merchant against what GME sent; a mismatch or missing record creates a `reconciliation_item` (DISCREPANCY / MISSING_SCHEME) and fires an ops alert; daily aggregate + USD-pool-identity tie-outs run on top.

4. **Onboarding → KYB → credentials → webhook.** During the config-registry onboarding wizard, step-3 **calls kyb-adapter** to screen the partner + UBOs (Stub/Octa) and verify business registration; the verdict is stored as evidence in `partner_kyb` (not an auto status flip — going ACTIVE is a 4-eyes operator decision). On activation config-registry **issues partner credentials** (bcrypt api-key in DB, HMAC secret in Vault; later rotated by the rotation scheduler; auth-identity resolves them at request time), **provisions the partner webhook** via notification-webhook, and **pushes the credit limit** to prefunding.

5. **Compliance filings.** Each cross-border commit produces a `bok_report_record` (FX1014/1015; domestic exempt). Schedulers aggregate and emit BOK exports, KoFIU CTR/STR AML feeds, and monthly Hometax tax invoices for gross-settlement merchant fees. Aggregation is live; the government transport channels are externally gated (OI-02/OI-03) and currently stubbed.

6. **Webhook delivery lifecycle.** Enqueue (trail step 7 WEBHOOK_QUEUED, atomic with the state change) → outbox poller signs + HTTPS-POSTs → 2xx = DELIVERED (trail step 8) → non-2xx/timeout = retry ladder 0/30/120/600/1800s then 3600s ×5 → after 10 failures DLQ + P2 alert; unsubscribed event types are short-circuited to DELIVERED without an HTTP call.

7. **Prefunding threshold breach.** When an OVERSEAS partner's balance drops below its configured threshold, prefunding emits `prefunding.low`; notification-webhook alerts the partner and ops. (Balance-driven auto-suspend of transacting is a config-registry/prefunding policy hook; the low-balance alert path is the wired trigger.)
