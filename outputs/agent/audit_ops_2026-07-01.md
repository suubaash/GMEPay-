# GMEPay+ Operational Gap Audit — Head of Operations
Date: 2026-07-01

The feature list is strong on *building* the payment rails and *booking* the money. It is thin on everything my team touches at 3am when a payment is stuck, a partner's float is empty, or a reconciliation won't tie out. Below are the genuine gaps — capabilities that exist to *run, watch, support and recover* the platform, not to process a happy-path payment. I have skipped anything already covered (state machine, DLQ, recon exception list, RBAC, audit chain, alerts-for-low-balance, etc.).

---

## A. Real-time monitoring & proactive alerting

1. **Live operations control tower (single pane).** One screen showing, right now: in-flight authorizes awaiting confirm, count/age of UNCERTAIN transactions, webhook backlog & DLQ depth, per-partner float headroom, scheme-connection health, FX-quote staleness. Today the data is scattered per-service. Ops needs one heartbeat view to know "are we healthy?" in 5 seconds.

2. **UNCERTAIN / stuck-transaction aging alerts.** The state machine *has* UNCERTAIN but nothing watches how long a txn sits there. Ops needs an alert when any txn (authorize-not-confirmed, pending-payout, mid-failover) exceeds an SLA threshold, so money isn't silently trapped between two rails.

3. **Float-breach *forecasting*, not just low-balance alerts.** Low-balance alert fires at a static threshold. Ops needs a *burn-rate / time-to-empty* projection ("Partner X float empties in ~40 min at current volume") so we top up *before* declines start, especially across timezone/weekend gaps when partners are asleep.

4. **Scheme & partner connectivity health board + circuit-breaker status.** Which schemes (ZeroPay/Khalti/Fonepay) and partner webhook endpoints are up, latency, error-rate, and whether a route is auto-tripped. Ops needs this to answer "is it us or them?" during an incident.

5. **Decline / failure-rate anomaly detection.** Alert on abnormal spikes in declines, FX-quote-expiry, or failover invocations by partner/scheme/direction — early warning of a broken integration or fraud, before partners call us.

## B. Daily operations workbench & queues

6. **Operator work-queue hub (assignable, SLA-tracked).** Unified inbox of exceptions: recon breaks, disputes, manual-refund requests, stuck txns, KYB approvals, float top-up approvals. With assignment, priority, ageing SLA, and "who's working it." Right now each is a scattered list; Ops needs a queue we can staff and measure.

7. **End-of-day / cutover operations console.** A guided EOD checklist: confirm all scheme files received, recon complete, float positions squared, revenue journals posted, open UNCERTAIN cleared or carried, sign-off. Cross-border across KST/NPT/USD cutoffs makes an explicit, auditable close essential.

8. **Position / float top-up *workflow* (request→approve→execute→confirm).** Prefunding mechanics exist, but not the *operational* top-up process with maker-checker, treasury notification, and confirmation that funds landed. This is a daily money-movement task, not a config value.

## C. Manual intervention & recovery tools

9. **Force-resolve UNCERTAIN toolkit.** Guided tool to query the scheme, then push a stuck txn to REVERSED or COMPLETED with mandatory reason + audit entry. Without this, an operator's only option is a DB edit — unacceptable for money.

10. **Manual webhook replay + recon re-run from the console.** Re-send a specific webhook (or a batch) and re-trigger reconciliation for a date/partner from the UI, not by engineering ticket. Ops must self-serve routine recovery.

11. **Emergency controls: suspend/quarantine partner, merchant, route, or scheme.** A big red button to instantly stop routing to a compromised partner/merchant/scheme with immediate effect and reason logging. Needed for fraud, outage, or compliance freeze.

12. **Kill-switch / global pause & maintenance mode.** Ability to pause new authorizations platform-wide (or per direction) while letting in-flight settle, for incident containment or scheme maintenance windows.

## D. Support: partner, merchant & customer

13. **360° transaction search & drill-down.** Search by any handle (txn ref, scheme ref, partner ref, customer id, amount, date-range) and see the full lifecycle, FX legs, journals, webhooks, and linked settlement. This is the #1 tool support uses on every partner call; today's "records" aren't described as searchable this way.

14. **Case / ticket management.** Log, track and resolve partner/merchant support cases linked to the underlying transaction(s), with status and history. Needed to run a support desk at all.

15. **Customer dispute / chargeback handling workflow.** Intake a disputed payment, freeze/investigate, decide, and drive the refund + partner notification + accounting reversal. Refund exists as a *function*; there is no *dispute process* wrapping it.

16. **Statement & report re-issue on demand.** Regenerate and re-deliver a partner/merchant statement for a period. Partners *will* ask for this monthly; without it, every request is an engineering job.

## E. Incident, SLA & governance

17. **Service-health / SLA dashboard with on-call alert routing.** Uptime, latency, error budgets per service and per external scheme, wired to PagerDuty/on-call rotation. Ops needs to know we're breaching SLA before the partner does.

18. **Runbook library linked to alerts.** Each alert type maps to a documented, versioned response procedure. Recovery must not depend on tribal knowledge.

19. **Operator-action audit *review* & reporting.** The hash-chained audit *stores* events; Ops/compliance need a searchable review UI over privileged operator actions (force-resolves, suspensions, manual top-ups) with periodic attestation. Storing ≠ supervising.

## F. Capacity, throughput & data

20. **Capacity / throughput & volume dashboard.** TPS, peak volumes, latency trends by scheme/partner/direction, and headroom vs. limits — for capacity planning and pre-empting rate-limit throttling of legitimate partners.

21. **Bulk operations & data export / self-service reporting.** Bulk refund/replay/statement generation and scheduled CSV/API exports for finance, treasury and partners. Manual one-at-a-time handling doesn't scale.

22. **Rate/pricing & FX-margin change *operational* controls.** Maker-checker workflow, effective-dating, and preview of impact when changing per partner×scheme×direction pricing or FX buffers. Pricing exists as config; changing live money-affecting rates needs guarded operational change control.
