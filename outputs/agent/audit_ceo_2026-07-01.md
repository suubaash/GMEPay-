# GMEPay+ Capstone Review — CEO Go/No-Go

## 1. Verdict

We have built an impressive **payments engine, not yet a payments business.** The rails move money end-to-end and pass tests, but "green in tests" is not "safe with real money" — roughly half of what reads as "done" is provisioned-not-plumbed (secrets are `CHANGE_ME`, migrations run in-app, metrics are dark, filings hit a stubbed gov channel), and we have **no AML/CFT program and no licence-examinable posture.** This is **not launchable today.** The single existential precondition before ANY real customer money moves: **we are legally licensed to move it in the corridor, with a functioning sanctions/AML program and an irrecoverable-ledger/backup guarantee — because those are the two ways this company dies, not the ways it grows slowly.**

Two death conditions, one growth condition. We do not touch live money until both death conditions are closed. We do not scale until the growth condition (unit economics) is proven.

## 2. Prioritization — Blockers vs Fast-Follow

### Top 5 MUST-DO-BEFORE-GO-LIVE (hard blockers, non-negotiable)

1. **Licence + live AML/CFT program.** Real sanctions/PEP screening on counterparties, ongoing transaction monitoring, SAR case workflow, Travel Rule, and a licensing register — filing against a *real* regulator channel, not a stub. Without this we are committing a crime, not launching a product. (Audit/Compliance)
2. **Irrecoverable-ledger fix + backups/PITR/DR.** Money ledgers with no tested restore path is a bet-the-company risk. Backups, point-in-time recovery, at least one rehearsed DR drill, and move DB migrations OUT of the app into a controlled, rollback-safe pipeline. (DevOps/CTO)
3. **Close the "provisioned-not-plumbed" gap on the critical path.** Real secrets/KMS (no `CHANGE_ME`), metrics + alerting + on-call live, batch jobs made multi-replica-safe. We must be able to *see* and *stop* the system in production. (CTO/DevOps)
4. **Ops control + kill-switch.** Live control tower, stuck-txn/float alerts, emergency suspend / global pause, and manual recovery (force-resolve/replay). If money gets stuck at 2am, a human must be able to see it and halt it. (Ops)
5. **One clean, reconciled, real-money end-to-end payment** — inbound→FX→payout→settlement→recon→GL — proven in a prod-parity environment with money that ties out to the cent. One real transaction that fully closes the loop beats a thousand green unit tests.

### Fast-follow (weeks after a gated live pilot, not before)
Finance depth (ERP export, treasury/hedging, month-end close, partner billing, tax) — needed early but can run semi-manual at pilot volume. Business Dev scaling engine (dev portal, SDKs, tiered pricing, CRM) — deliberately deferred; **do not build for scale before proving one corridor makes money.** CTO scaling (scheme-adapter SDK, horizontal HA, event governance) — the growth flywheel, sequenced after the pilot survives.

## 3. Strategy-Level Gaps NO Department Owns (I am adding these)

- **Launch-Readiness Scorecard — "code-exists" vs "proven-in-prod."** Every capability rated on that axis, not a binary done/not-done. This directly attacks the "half of done is a lie" problem. Go-live requires green on the scorecard, not green in the test suite.
- **A single accountable Go-Live Owner.** One named executive owns the gate and can say no. Right now readiness is diffused across six heads — which means no one owns it.
- **Phased country rollout with regulatory gates.** One corridor, capped volume, capped ticket size, real regulator sign-off before the next corridor opens. No big-bang launch.
- **Unit-economics visibility BEFORE scaling.** We may not currently know if we make money per corridor after float cost, FX, and fees. Profitability-per-corridor is a *gate to scale*, not a finance nicety.
- **Trust/brand + customer-harm plan.** A stuck or lost payment is a lost customer and a headline. Define the support, refund, and comms posture before the first customer.
- **Contingency / kill-switch + wind-down plan.** Pre-agreed triggers to pause the corridor and unwind cleanly, protecting customer funds first.

## 4. The Board / Regulator Narrative (honest)

**What is real:** We have built a genuinely strong cross-border payment *platform* — inbound and outbound rails with failover, FX, prefunding, settlement, reconciliation, revenue journals, onboarding/KYB, routing, and a cloud-agnostic deployment. The engineering core is real and tested, and the money-flow logic is sound.

**What is aspirational:** It is not yet an *operable, examinable, licensed business.* We have compliance plumbing but not a live AML/CFT or privacy program; infrastructure is designed but not hardened (secrets, backups, DR, observability); and a meaningful share of "complete" features are wired for demos, not production.

**The plan:** We are treating this as pre-launch, not launch. We will not move one dollar of customer money until (a) we are licensed with a working AML program, and (b) our ledger is provably recoverable. We will then run a single, volume-capped corridor under regulatory gates, prove it is safe *and* profitable, and only then invest in the scaling engine. We would rather launch one corridor late and clean than five corridors fast and fragile. The existential risks here are regulatory and ledger integrity — and we are refusing to launch until both are closed.
