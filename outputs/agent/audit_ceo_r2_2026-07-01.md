# GMEPay+ — CEO Round-2 Capstone

## 1. Does round 2 change the picture?
Yes — and not in our favour. Building the Operations layer (4.17) did not move us toward launch; it added a class of live, money-affecting risk we did not have before, because we shipped operator superpowers (force-resolve, recon re-run, suspend) without the controls that make them safe. The primitives compile and demo, but the money-critical paths behind them are wrong: reversals move money with no ledger entry, RBAC and the kill-switch both fail *open*, and the event lineage that revenue recognition depends on is silently split in two. **Meta-lesson: this team builds the happy-path surface convincingly and skips the invariants — so "it's built" keeps meaning "it demos," not "it's safe."**

## 2. The critical reframe
Stop calling these "missing features." Four of the round-2 findings are **defects in code we just merged**:
- **Unledgered reversals** — force-resolve→REVERSED changes the money outcome and never books a journal or releases held float.
- **RBAC fails open** — the guard on money-affecting actions grants on failure.
- **Split event lineage** — operator terminal states emit only the FSM event, so `payment.*` consumers (revenue, notifications) never fire.
- **No ShedLock → single-replica** — a correctness bug that pins the whole platform to one instance (no HA).

These are not backlog items; they are regressions in shipped code and they **must be fixed before any new feature work**. They vindicate the round-1 scorecard exactly: "code-exists" was never the bar — "proven-in-prod" was, and round 2 is the proof that the gap is real and dangerous.

## 3. Top 5 MUST-FIX-NOW (freeze everything else)
1. **Fix the ledger + event lineage together.** Operator terminal states must emit the domain `payment.*` events; REVERSED must book a reversing journal and release the held float; COMPLETED must recognise revenue. Money must not move without a matching ledger entry — this is the launch-blocking invariant.
2. **Fail-CLOSED RBAC + route money-affecting actions through the existing four-eyes engine.** No bespoke approval — reuse what the platform already has. Deny on guard failure.
3. **ShedLock on all schedulers before we run >1 replica.** No HA until scheduled jobs are single-fire-safe.
4. **Fail-CLOSED kill-switch + config-registry HA and hard timeout.** A suspended partner must not transact during a registry outage; the registry cannot be an untimed pay-path SPOF.
5. **Close the alert loop to a real pager.** `gmepay.ops.alert` needs a consumer and a paging sink; wire FLOAT_LOW / DECLINE_SPIKE emitters. An alert nobody receives is not a control.

## 4. The strategic call
**Freeze feature-adding. Harden the base.** Order: no new department features, no new operator actions, no new lanes until the five above are fixed, tested, and demonstrated in a multi-replica staging run. The audit rounds have done their job — we now know the shape of the debt. Adding more surface on top of fail-open controls and unledgered money only deepens the hole. Business Dev's partner-notification/status-page and the 503 docs come *after* the base is safe, not alongside.

## 5. Board narrative
Round 2 delivered the Operations backend and, in doing so, gave us the clearest picture yet of where we stand: the platform is feature-rich and demo-ready, but several money-critical controls we just shipped are defective — reversals that don't touch the ledger, access and kill-switch guards that fail open, and a split event lineage that silently drops revenue recognition. None of this makes GMEPay+ closer to launch; it confirms the round-1 thesis that "built" and "safe to run with real money" are different milestones, and we had been counting the wrong one. The team's velocity is real and the primitives are sound, so the fix is disciplined, not existential: I am freezing new feature work and directing all engineering to close five must-fix defects, with launch gated on passing a multi-replica staging run against them. Expect no new capabilities next cycle — and a materially more trustworthy platform at the end of it.
